Social Causes of Inequality

Social inequality limits opportunities for education, healthcare, and fair treatment. Below are some of the most pressing issues.

Poor Access to Quality Education

Rural schools lack proper facilities, teachers, and funding. Many students walk long distances to attend classes. Urban private schools provide advanced programs, creating a large learning gap between rural and city students.

Shortage of Healthcare Services

Provinces suffer from understaffed hospitals and lack of doctors. Poor families often rely on barangay health workers with limited supplies, leaving them vulnerable in times of crisis.

Discrimination Against Indigenous Peoples

Indigenous groups face exclusion from jobs, education, and healthcare. Many are displaced from their ancestral lands, further worsening poverty and inequality.

Gender Inequality

Women often earn less and have fewer leadership opportunities. They also bear the burden of unpaid household work alongside employment, limiting their advancement.

Urban-Rural Divide

Cities enjoy better infrastructure and opportunities, while rural areas are left behind. This fuels migration to urban centers and causes brain drain in the provinces.

Limited Social Mobility

Children from poor families struggle to break the cycle of poverty due to malnutrition, poor education, and lack of access to stable jobs.
