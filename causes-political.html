Political Causes of Inequality

Political systems and governance play a crucial role in shaping inequality. Corruption and favoritism have long-term impacts on the distribution of wealth and resources.

Corruption in Government

Public funds meant for healthcare, education, and infrastructure are often misused. This reduces access to essential services and weakens trust in institutions.

Unequal Representation in Politics

Political dynasties dominate elections. As a result, laws and policies often favor wealthy elites, leaving marginalized communities unheard.

Weak Implementation of Pro-Poor Laws

Programs like PhilHealth and the 4Ps suffer from bureaucracy and corruption, limiting their intended benefits for the poor.

Centralized Governance

Most resources are concentrated in Metro Manila, while provinces are neglected. Local governments often lack funds for development and disaster response.

Patronage Politics

Politicians often distribute benefits based on loyalty rather than need. This creates unfair systems where ordinary citizens are left behind.

Peace and Security Issues

Armed conflicts in Mindanao disrupt economic growth, displace families, and discourage investments, worsening regional inequality.
