Environmental Causes of Inequality

Natural disasters and climate change heavily impact poor Filipinos who lack resources to recover. Infrastructure gaps make communities even more vulnerable.

Natural Disasters

Typhoons, floods, and earthquakes affect millions yearly. Poor families suffer the most as they live in unsafe housing and cannot easily rebuild after disasters.

Climate Change

Rising sea levels and unpredictable weather hurt farmers and fishers. Rural communities face food insecurity as agriculture becomes less stable.

Poor Infrastructure in Disaster Areas

Lack of strong roads, bridges, and evacuation centers slows recovery after disasters. Rural communities are left isolated for days or weeks during emergencies.
