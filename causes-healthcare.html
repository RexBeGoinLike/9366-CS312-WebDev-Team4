Healthcare Access Inequality

Access to healthcare in the Philippines is highly unequal, with rural areas and poor families left behind.

Urban vs. Rural Divide

Most hospitals and specialized facilities are concentrated in Metro Manila and other big cities, leaving rural Filipinos with limited access to doctors and essential services. This forces many families to travel long distances or forego treatment.

High Cost of Medical Care

Even with PhilHealth, many Filipinos cannot afford quality healthcare. Out-of-pocket expenses for medicine, hospital stays, and treatments push families into debt, especially those already living in poverty.

Shortage of Healthcare Workers

Many Filipino doctors and nurses migrate abroad for better pay, leaving local hospitals understaffed. This healthcare worker shortage especially affects rural communities, making services inconsistent and often inaccessible.
