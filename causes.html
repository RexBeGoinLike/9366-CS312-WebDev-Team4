Main Causes of Inequality

Inequality in the Philippines has deep roots. Below are the biggest contributors:

  • Income Inequality: The top 10% of families earn over 30% of national income, while the bottom 20% share less than 5%.
  • Education Gap: Children from poor families often drop out early and lack access to quality schools.
  • Healthcare Disparities: Rural areas lack hospitals, while urban centers have better access to doctors and medicines.
  • Regional Divide: Luzon is far more developed than Mindanao, creating unequal opportunities for jobs and infrastructure.
  • Gender Inequality: Women often earn less than men and face barriers in leadership roles.
  • Corruption and Governance: Weak institutions and misuse of funds limit social protection programs.

Cycle of Poverty

Many poor families remain poor for generations because they cannot afford education, healthcare, or capital to start businesses. This creates a cycle of poverty that is difficult to break without strong government intervention and social programs.
