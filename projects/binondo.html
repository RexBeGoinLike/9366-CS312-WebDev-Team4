Binondo–Intramuros Bridge Completed

By TransparencyPH Staff | Published April 5, 2022

The Binondo–Intramuros Bridge is a modern 680-meter, 4-lane cable-stayed bridge that connects the historic districts of Binondo and Intramuros in Manila. It eases congestion across the Pasig River and strengthens cultural and economic connectivity.

Project Information

  • Location: Manila, Metro Manila
  • Status: ✅ Completed (April 2022)
  • Contractor: China Road and Bridge Corporation
  • Budget: ₱3.39 billion
  • Timeline: 2018–2022

Background

Built under the “Build, Build, Build” program during the Duterte administration, the bridge was co-financed through a grant from the Chinese government. Despite controversies regarding its impact on heritage areas, the bridge was inaugurated on April 5, 2022.

Impact

  • Reduced travel time between Binondo and Intramuros to under 5 minutes.
  • Improved logistics for businesses in Manila’s Chinatown.
  • Provided an alternative crossing to the Jones Bridge.
