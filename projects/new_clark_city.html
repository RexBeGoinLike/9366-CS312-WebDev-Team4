New Clark City: Building the Philippines’ Green and Smart Metropolis

By TransparencyPH Staff | Updated September 2025

New Clark City (NCC) is envisioned as the Philippines’ first smart, green, and disaster-resilient metropolis. Located in Capas, Tarlac, within the Clark Special Economic Zone, it is designed to decongest Metro Manila and provide a new center for government, business, and innovation.

Project Information

  • Location: Capas, Tarlac (within Clark Freeport and Special Economic Zone)
  • Status: Phase 1 completed; expansion phases ongoing
  • Land Area: 9,450 hectares
  • Key Facilities: Government Administrative Center, Athletics Stadium, Aquatics Center
  • Budget: ₱1.2 trillion (multi-phase, long-term development)
  • Implementing Agency: Bases Conversion and Development Authority (BCDA)
  • Timeline: 2016–2065 (planned build-out)

Background & Rationale

Metro Manila faces overpopulation, congestion, flooding, and seismic risks. New Clark City was conceptualized to provide a modern alternative, spreading development to Central Luzon while boosting resilience against natural disasters. The site’s strategic location—accessible via expressways, Clark Airport, and future railways—makes it ideal for becoming a new growth hub.

Phase 1 Achievements

  • Completion of world-class sports facilities used in the 2019 Southeast Asian Games.
  • Establishment of the National Government Administrative Center (NGAC) to house backup offices for government continuity during disasters.
  • Initial residential and commercial developments to accommodate workers and residents.

Economic & Social Impact

New Clark City is expected to attract investments in IT, manufacturing, logistics, and green industries. It will host hundreds of thousands of residents and workers in a master-planned environment with integrated public transport, renewable energy, and open green spaces.

Environmental & Community Considerations

The project emphasizes sustainability through solar farms, waste-to-energy facilities, water recycling, and smart-grid infrastructure. Resettlement programs for local communities, including Aeta groups, are ongoing with livelihood and housing support provided by the government.

Future Outlook

By 2065, New Clark City is projected to be home to over 1 million people and serve as a model for sustainable urbanization in Southeast Asia. Its integration with the North–South Commuter Railway and Clark International Airport will strengthen its role as a regional hub.
