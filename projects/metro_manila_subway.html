Metro Manila Subway: The Philippines’ First Underground Rail System

By TransparencyPH Staff | Updated September 2025

The Metro Manila Subway Project (MMSP) is the country’s first-ever underground mass transit system. Once operational, it will revolutionize commuting in the capital by reducing travel time between Quezon City and Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA) from more than an hour to just 35 minutes.

Project Information

  • Location: Metro Manila (Quezon City to NAIA and Bicutan, Taguig)
  • Status: Under construction (partial operations targeted by 2027)
  • Length: 33 kilometers (Phase 1)
  • Stations: 17 planned stations, with provisions for expansion
  • Budget: ₱488.5 billion (largest infrastructure investment in Philippine history)
  • Funding: Supported by Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA) with concessional loans
  • Contractors: Japanese and Filipino joint ventures for tunneling and station works

Background & Rationale

Metro Manila is one of the most congested cities in the world, with commuters often spending 2–3 hours in traffic daily. The subway is a cornerstone of the government’s transport modernization agenda, designed to provide a reliable, safe, and efficient alternative to road-based transport.

Construction Highlights & Challenges

The project uses advanced tunnel boring machines (TBMs) imported from Japan. Construction is ongoing for several key stations, including those in Valenzuela, Quezon City, and Ortigas. Despite delays caused by the pandemic and right-of-way issues, progress continues steadily.

Engineering challenges include Metro Manila’s soft soil conditions, existing underground utilities, and flood risks. To address these, stations are designed with waterproofing systems and seismic-resilient features.

Economic & Social Impact

  • Capacity: up to 1.5 million passengers per day when fully operational.
  • Drastic reduction in travel time across Metro Manila.
  • Job creation for thousands of engineers, construction workers, and service providers.
  • Expected to raise productivity by reducing time lost to traffic congestion.

Environmental & Community Considerations

The subway incorporates energy-efficient systems, modern ventilation, and flood management technology. Resettlement programs have been rolled out for affected households, with compensation packages and relocation support in line with JICA safeguards.

Future Outlook

Partial operations covering several northern stations are expected by 2027, with full line completion projected around 2032. The system is envisioned as the backbone of an integrated urban rail network, linking with LRT-1, MRT-3, and the North–South Commuter Railway.
